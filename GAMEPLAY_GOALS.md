# Gameplay Goals

## Player experience target

The game should feel like a readable, grounded, dark ARPG dungeon crawler.

The player experience should be:

- readable over flashy
- rewarding over noisy
- tense but fair
- mobile-friendly without feeling shallow

## Intended feel

- dungeon rooms should feel distinct and memorable
- treasure should feel earned
- enemies should communicate danger clearly
- room identity should be readable at a glance
- progression should encourage one more room, one more level, one more drop

## Combat goals

- combat should feel responsive, readable, and dangerous enough to matter
- enemies should support category identity over time:
  - beasts
  - humanoids
  - bosses
- loot and encounter density should feel intentional, not random clutter

## Dungeon goals

- rooms should vary in purpose, not just shape
- corridors should support pacing and anticipation
- stairs should create a satisfying descent structure
- archetypes should create stories:
  - storage room
  - bone room
  - chest room
  - rat nest
  - torch hall
  - dead-end loot nook

## Visual goals

- dark, readable, torch-lit spaces
- clear floor/wall separation
- props that read instantly
- character and equipment visuals should support progression fantasy

## Design guardrails

- do not over-randomize reward placement
- do not sacrifice readability for decoration
- do not let art or systems drift back toward a cute/chibi overworld RPG feel
