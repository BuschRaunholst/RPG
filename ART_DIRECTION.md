# Art Direction

## High-level visual goal

The game should feel like a dark ARPG / dungeon crawler rather than a cute chibi RPG.

Key inspirations:

- early Diablo mood
- grounded pixel-art dungeon spaces
- readable silhouettes
- dark stone interiors with warm torch contrast

## Art rules

- Characters should feel more human and less cute/chibi.
- Dungeon visuals should be moody, readable, and slightly grim.
- Props should support gameplay readability first.
- Lighting should help orientation, not wash the dungeon in glow.
- Treasure should feel rewarding and visually distinct.

## Character direction

- Player is modular: body, armor, boots, weapon, shield.
- Humanoid proportions are taller/skinnier than the old cute RPG style.
- Equipment should affect the visual character where possible.

## Dungeon direction

- Stone floor with readable wear, cracks, moss, and variation.
- Wall caps and side walls should be consistent and easy to parse.
- Black void outside walkable space should stay clean and readable.
- Room archetypes should be recognizable at a glance.

## Prop direction

- Barrels, bones, nests, torches, rubble, and chests should read clearly in one glance.
- Chests should feel deliberate and rewarding, not randomly sprinkled.
- Rat nests should feel dirty, alive, and dangerous.

## What to avoid

- Cute, overly bright, toy-like assets
- noisy art that hurts readability
- floating details that visually detach from props
- visual fixes that hide broken geometry instead of solving it
